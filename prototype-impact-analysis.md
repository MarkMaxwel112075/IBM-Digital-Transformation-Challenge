# Prototype Impact Analysis — Beyond B
## Testing Results & Metric Comparisons | MG5661 Item 2 | 🏆 Distinction

---

## Overview

Prototype testing compared Beyond B against B University's current
UCAS-based application method across four key dimensions: application
completion likelihood, perceived ease, course change rate, and overall
engagement. Results informed the final consultancy recommendations.

---

## 1. Application Completion Likelihood

**"How likely are you to complete the application using this method?"**

| Method | Very Likely to Complete |
|---|---|
| Beyond B (Gamified Prototype) | ~90% |
| Current Application Method | ~60% |
| **Improvement** | **↑ 30–40 percentage points** |

> Beyond B produced a significant uplift in student willingness to
> complete the full application journey — directly addressing B University's
> dropout problem.

---

## 2. Perceived Ease of Application

**"How easy did you find this application process?"**

| Method | Rated "Easy" or "Very Easy" |
|---|---|
| Beyond B (Gamified Prototype) | ~80% |
| Current Application Method | ~35% |
| **Improvement** | **↑ ~45 percentage points** |

> The Quest Map structure and step-by-step guidance dramatically reduced
> the cognitive load students experienced, consistent with Huang,
> Hew & Lo (2020).

---

## 3. Projected Course Change Rate

**Trait mapping impact on course switching behaviour**

| Method | Projected Course Change Rate |
|---|---|
| Current Student Course Change Rate | ~30% |
| Beyond B Projected Rate | ~8% |
| **Improvement** | **↓ ~75% reduction** |

> Career Trait Quiz results correlated strongly with course fit confidence.
> Students who completed trait mapping reported significantly higher
> certainty about their course choice (Ming, Richards & Kettlewell, 2023;
> Kim & Lee, 2022).

---

## 4. Overall Engagement & Excitement

**"How would you describe your experience with this application method?"**

| Response | Beyond B | Current Method |
|---|---|---|
| Fun / Engaging | Large majority | Minority |
| Neutral / Boring | Small minority | Large majority |

> The gamified elements — XP system, avatar builder, mini-games, and
> quest structure — created an emotionally engaging experience that
> resonated strongly with Gen Z participants (Goh & Yang, 2021).

---

## 5. BI Dashboard — Admissions Team Impact

Beyond B's integrated BI dashboard enabled admissions staff to shift
from reactive to proactive decision-making:

| Capability | Before Beyond B | After Beyond B |
|---|---|---|
| Application volume visibility | End-of-cycle reports | Real-time dashboard |
| Student engagement tracking | Not available | Weekly quiz plays, saves |
| Course interest patterns | Manual analysis | Automated trend tracking |
| Acceptance rate monitoring | Monthly reports | Live KPI display |
| Decision-making approach | Reactive | Proactive and data-driven |

---

## 6. Stakeholder Feedback — Key Themes

### Positive
- Strong visual design and intuitive navigation
- Career quiz seen as genuinely useful for undecided students
- BI dashboard received strong interest from admissions team
- Mini-games praised for making career exploration engaging

### Areas for Development (Identified During Q&A)
- Integration with existing university systems needs scoping
- Data privacy framework needs explicit documentation
- Accessibility features required for students with disabilities
- Scalability plan needed for high-traffic application periods

---

## 7. Lessons for Future Prototype Testing

| Learning | Action for Next Iteration |
|---|---|
| Test with broader demographic sample | Include students with lower digital confidence |
| Validate aesthetics before building | Run preference surveys before design phase |
| Assess organisational feasibility early | Map IT infrastructure requirements in Week 1 |
| Embed ethics from the start | Run ethical impact assessment before prototyping |
| Use low-fidelity first | Test Quest Map logic with sketches before building |

---

## Conclusion

Prototype testing confirmed that Beyond B materially outperforms the
current application method on every measured dimension. The platform
demonstrates strong potential to increase application completion rates,
reduce course switching, and improve the admissions team's operational
effectiveness through real-time BI analytics.

Implementation success will depend on addressing organisational
feasibility, data governance, and accessibility requirements
identified during stakeholder Q&A.
