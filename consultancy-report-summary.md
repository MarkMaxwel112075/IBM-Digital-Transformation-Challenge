# Consultancy Report — Commercial Readiness Gap at Ceres Power
## MG5661 Item 1 | 🏆 Distinction | Word Count: 2,059

## 1. Introduction
Ceres Power Holdings plc develops SOFC and SOEC technologies under a licensing model. Partners including Bosch, Doosan, and Weichai manufacture and commercialise products using Ceres' IP. Despite strong technical credentials, Ceres fails to present a coherent commercial story compared to Bloom Energy, SolidPower, and Ningbo SOFC.

## 2. Three Core Barriers
**Barrier 1 — Inconsistent Reporting:** Partners use varied formats and timelines; forecasting relies on assumptions not evidence (Ly et al., 2021).
**Barrier 2 — Lack of Digital Signalling:** No public metrics or deployment updates; absent signals create greater uncertainty than actual gaps (Saura et al., 2022).
**Barrier 3 — Partner Readiness Variability:** No standardised readiness assessment; literature confirms structured governance frameworks are essential (Kohtamäki et al., 2020).

## 4. Strategic Recommendations
1. **Unified BI Platform** — Centralise partner data for forecasting and reporting (Gupta & George, 2020)
2. **Digital Signalling Strategy** — Publish quarterly anonymised deployment updates
3. **Tiered Partner Readiness Model** — 3-tier governance: High Readiness / Development / Exploratory
4. **Phased Rollout** — Templates → BI Dashboard → Public Updates → Partner Reviews
